  • 1. What is the 'double diamond' approach? What are the four phases and a short description of each phase?

    • The double diamond approach is an industry standard that is used to develop systems. The 4 phases are: discover, define, develop, deliver. These are based around discovering the problem and those involved, gathering information to create a solution, developing the solution with prototypes and tests, and delivering the product as described

  • 3. What is "cooperative design" and how does it fit in with what you know about Agile?

    • Cooperative design is a method that aims to include many people working on the same system. It prioritizes certain methods mainly, collaboration, flexibility, and adaptability meant to have the design and integration process happen simultaneously.


  • 4. What are Gould & Lewis's three key principles of HCI.

    • The principles are visibility, feedbackm abd constraints. These are guidlines meant to give user flexibility.


  • 5. What are the four basic activities of interaction design?

    • Research, specification, design, evaluation. These include ideas of understanding the users goals, the requirements needed, the visual and sensory attributes, and the the ensuring of user requirements and changes.
